## What You're Doing

### Step 1: Select the Best Blog Idea

Look at the 6 blog post ideas from Stage 2. Pick the ONE that would make the strongest standalone article.

Consider:
- **Searchability** - Would someone Google this?
- **Specificity** - Is there a clear angle, not a vague topic?
- **Usefulness** - Can readers actually do something with this?
- **Distinctiveness** - Does this stand out from generic content?

Explain briefly why you chose this one over the others.

---

### Step 2: Create Two Article Outlines

You're creating outlines for TWO articles. Both should be ~750 words.

---

#### Article 1: Episode Recap (promotes the podcast episode)

This article summarizes and promotes the episode. Someone reading it should understand what the episode covers, get some value from the summary, and want to listen.

Structure:
- **Hook** (75-100 words): Open with the core tension or insight from the episode. Not a rhetorical question. Not "In this post, I'll..."
- **What This Episode Covers** (150-200 words): A narrative overview of the key themes and moments
- **Key Insights** (200-250 words): 2-3 specific insights or takeaways from the conversation
- **Why Listen** (75-100 words): What makes this episode worth someone's time
- **Episode Link/CTA** (50-75 words): Direct them to listen

Include notes on which quotes from Stage 2 to integrate.

---

#### Article 2: Topic Article (standalone piece)

This article is based on your selected blog idea. It should be useful and complete on its own. Someone reading it doesn't need to know this came from a podcast episode.

Structure:
- **Hook** (75-100 words): Open strong. Name a problem, share a counterintuitive insight, or start with a specific moment.
- **Context** (100-150 words): Why this matters, what's at stake, common misconceptions
- **Main Sections** (400-450 words across 3 sections): Each section needs a clear purpose. Balance insight with practical application.
- **Takeaway** (75-100 words): One clear thing to think about or try. Not a summary. Not a hard sell.

Include notes on which quotes and tips from Stage 2 to use.

---

## Style Guardrails

Write like a human who understands this audience. Be specific.

Avoid:
- Rhetorical questions as hooks
- "In today's world..."
- Vague section titles like "Why It Matters" (be specific about WHAT matters)
- Generic advice that could apply to any topic
- "Delve," "navigate," "unpack," "unlock," "transformative"
