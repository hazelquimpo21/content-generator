## Task

Create 5 Instagram post captions to promote this blog post. Each should work as a standalone piece of content that drives curiosity about the episode topic.

## Instagram Voice Guidelines

**The Instagram voice is:**
- Personal and confessional ("the thing about X is—")
- Mid-thought, like you're processing something out loud
- Lowercase energy (not literally, but tonally)
- Uses em-dashes liberally
- Feels like a DM from a friend who happens to be a therapist

**Sentence patterns that work:**
- "Nobody talks about how [specific observation]"
- "The part that gets me is—"
- "Something I've been sitting with lately:"
- "Here's what I wish someone had told me about [topic]"

## Quality Framework

A good Instagram caption:
✓ First line is the hook (gets cut off after ~125 chars—make it count)
✓ Feels like a voice memo transcribed
✓ Has one clear idea, not three
✓ Creates "that's so true" recognition
✓ Doesn't beg for engagement
✓ Hashtags feel curated, not SEO-stuffed

## Generate 5 Posts

Create variety across these lengths:
1. **Short** (100-150 characters) — punchy, quotable, one idea
2. **Short** (100-150 characters) — different angle or hook type
3. **Medium** (250-400 characters) — more context, still tight
4. **Medium** (250-400 characters) — different approach
5. **Long** (500-800 characters) — fuller story or explanation, carousel-style

## Hook Types (use variety)

- **Bold claim**: State something surprising or counterintuitive
- **Recognition**: Name something your audience feels but hasn't articulated
- **Story opener**: Start in the middle of a moment
- **Reframe**: Challenge a common assumption
- **Question**: Only if genuinely thought-provoking (not "Who else feels...")

## Prohibited Content

**Never use:**
- "Link in bio!" as the hook
- Emoji walls or excessive emojis (1-2 max per post)
- "Who else feels this way?" or similar engagement bait
- "Drop a [emoji] if you relate!"
- Generic motivational language
- Starting every post with "I"
- Therapy clichés: "your feelings are valid," "it's okay not to be okay," "give yourself grace"
- AI tells: "delve," "navigate," "unpack," "in today's world"

**Hashtag rules:**
- 3-5 hashtags per post
- Mix broad (#mentalhealth) and specific (#anxietyrelief, #therapistsofinstagram)
- No made-up hashtags
- Place at end, not inline
